Intro

My name is Mehdi Aminazadeh. I was born on April 18, 2000, in Lar, Iran. I am funny and cool, and I love nature and travelling. I am also driven to be the best, thanks to my perfectionism, though I try to keep it in check. As my friends often say, I am known for my straightforward attitude, especially with phrases like: "Don’t play with me if you don’t go straight to the point". This website represents all the work and accomplishments I have achieved throughout my academic and sports careers.

About

I started my basketball career at 11 years old, under the guidance of Mr. Mehdi Amiraghajan. I continued playing as a point guard and small forward for Isfahan teams like Espadana, Reef, Sepahan Tozi, and the Isfahan province team. Thanks to my coach, Ali Khosravi, and his valuable training sessions, I made it to the Zobahan Superleague team. Eventually, I was invited to join Iran’s national basketball team, where I trained and played friendly games against Georgia and other regional teams. However, due to some "rich kids and their fathers," I was cut from the team. Left with the choice between sports and studies, I focused on my education at PNU. With no regrets about leaving basketball, I reached a good standing in my major. I am not a geek, but I try my best. You can find out what happened next on my CV from the previous tab. I am also interested in investing—whether it is in stock markets, real estate, or similar areas. I do not make risky plays, so do not try to convince me to invest in your low-market-cap business. I am not the type to put money into small, unknown companies with unproven cash-generating patterns. After reading books by Ittelson, Graham, Damodaran, and other greats, I have learned not to be fooled by flashy ideas that make no sense in taking down competitors. As the great Benjamin Franklin once said: "I conceive that the great part of the miseries of mankind are brought upon them by false estimates they have made of the value of things," I also love diving into financial statements. On the programming side, I have completed projects for the Isfahan City Municipality and a Danesh Bonyan company in the Shiraz Industrial Town. Thankfully, they went well! You can access the codes on the Contact tab, but I believe it is useless to take a look at, as you do not really know their logic. It is there only to showcase that I am a random coder.
